Introduction

A Dialog is a modal window appearing above page contents to provide critical information or asking users to confirm a specific action. Thus, to focus users attention, all other functionalities have to be unreachable and the Dialog must stay on screen until it is proceed.

Dialogs block user's flow, so they should be used sparingly and wisely.

Takeaways

  • A dialog focus user attention to ensure confirmation about a key step, often led by a user action
  • Dialog content should be straight and to the point when sharing information and guiding to proceed a specific task.
  • Dialog has to be part of a helpful process by carrying relevant and contextual information.

Pattern

  • When a Dialog is displayed, no other interactions on the page is allowed.
  • A Backdrop has to cover the content beneath the Dialog in order to bring focus to its content, and prevent users trying to click on the page.
  • If time is needed to process the primary action, the Button can display a loader. No actions are displayed and it loads until the action is complete.
